Events

Friday: September 13, 2019

Mehndi and Raas/Garba

The Raas/Garba is an evening of traditional Indian dance and music.

Garba

Garba is a folk dance, originating from the state of Gujarat, where everyone dances in concentric circles. There are simple steps (which are easy to learn on the spot), and more complex ones. These steps are repeated over and over in a circle.

Raas

Raas is a partnered dance, also originating from the state of Gujarat, that involves two sticks called dandiya. The steps are easy to learn, and you can jump in and out. It’s very easy to learn on the spot.

Mehndi

Mehndi also known as henna is a temporary skin tattoo traditionally designed on the hands and/or feet of women. Everyone, men and women, are welcome to get a small design during this event. The paste hardens overtime on your skin and can be washed or wiped off leaving a non-permanent tattoo that slowly fades over 2 weeks.

Attire

Men typically wear Kurta/Pajamas and women wear “chunia-choli” or “lengha”. If you don’t have indian clothes, men can wear business casual. Women can wear long skirts and a fun top.

Saturday: September 14, 2019

Wedding

There are two parts to the wedding. The first part is the baraat, where the groom arrives, and the second part is the seated ceremony.

Attire

You can wear either Indian traditional or formal American attire. Women can wear a saree, lehenga, or salwar and men can wear a kurta or an indowestern.

Reception

There will be a cocktail hour followed by a reception party.

Attire

The reception will be formal. You can wear either Indian or American. Women can wear long formal dresses or salwars, and men can wear suits or indowestern.
